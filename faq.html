FAQs

Q: What are the hospital’s visiting hours?

A: Visiting hours are from 9:00 AM to 8:00 PM daily. However, access to critical care units such as the ICU and CCU may be restricted to specific times. This helps us maintain patient safety and provide a healing environment.

Q: How can I book an appointment with a doctor?

A: Appointments can be booked online through our hospital’s website, by calling our reception desk, or in person at the OPD counter. We recommend booking in advance to minimize waiting times and ensure the availability of your preferred doctor.

Q: Does the hospital accept emergency cases 24/7?

A: Yes, our Emergency Department operates 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. A team of doctors, nurses, and paramedics is always available to handle critical cases promptly and efficiently.

Q: What insurance providers does the hospital accept?

A: We accept most major national and international insurance plans. Patients are advised to contact our billing department before admission to confirm coverage details and ensure a smooth cashless process.

Q: How can I get a copy of my medical records?

A: Medical records can be obtained from the Medical Records Department upon submitting a request form and valid identification. Please allow 2–3 working days for processing to ensure accuracy and confidentiality.

Q: Are visitors allowed to bring food for patients?

A: Visitors may bring food only with prior approval from the patient’s physician or dietitian. This ensures the diet is safe and appropriate for the patient’s treatment plan and recovery process.

Q: Does the hospital have a pharmacy?

A: Yes, we have a 24/7 in-house pharmacy located on the hospital premises. It provides prescription medicines, medical supplies, and other essentials for both inpatients and outpatients.

Q: How can I contact the hospital in case of an emergency?

A: In emergencies, you can call our dedicated emergency hotline available on the Contact Us page. Alternatively, you may dial the main hospital number for immediate assistance and ambulance support.

Q: Is there a blood bank in the hospital?

A: Yes, our hospital maintains a licensed and fully functional blood bank. It is equipped to provide safe, screened blood and blood products for surgeries, emergencies, and critical treatments.

Q: What facilities are available for patient attendants?

A: We provide comfortable waiting lounges, cafeteria services, prayer rooms, and restrooms for attendants. Our goal is to ensure attendants feel cared for while their loved ones are receiving treatment.

Q: Does the hospital offer ambulance services?

A: Yes, our hospital has a fleet of fully equipped ambulances available 24/7. Each ambulance is staffed with trained paramedics and fitted with life-support systems to handle emergencies effectively.

Q: How do I know which specialist to consult?

A: Our reception and help desk staff will guide you to the appropriate specialist based on your symptoms or medical history. In some cases, a general physician may first assess your condition before referring you to a specialist.

Q: Does the hospital provide vaccination services?

A: Yes, we offer routine immunizations for children and adults, as well as specialized travel vaccines. Our vaccination center operates during OPD hours and ensures safe, WHO-approved vaccination practices.

Q: Is Wi-Fi available in the hospital?

A: Yes, complimentary Wi-Fi is available in patient rooms and waiting areas. This allows patients and attendants to stay connected with family, work, or entertainment during their hospital stay.
